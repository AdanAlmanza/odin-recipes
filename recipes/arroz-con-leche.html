Arroz con leche

Description:

Arroz con Leche is a traditional Mexican dessert made with rice, milk, sugar, and cinnamon. Itâ€™s a creamy, comforting dish that can be enjoyed warm or cold. The subtle sweetness and spiced aroma make it a perfect treat for any time of the day.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup long-grain white rice
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 4 cups whole milk
  • 1 cup evaporated milk
  • 1 cup sweetened condensed milk
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Ground cinnamon (for garnish)
  • Raisins (optional, for topping)

Steps:

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine the rice, water, and cinnamon stick. Bring to a boil over medium heat.
  2. Once boiling, reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer until the rice is tender and the water is absorbed, about 15 minutes.
  3. Add the whole milk, evaporated milk, and sweetened condensed milk to the saucepan with the cooked rice. Stir well.
  4. Simmer the mixture over low heat, stirring frequently to prevent sticking, until it thickens and becomes creamy (about 20-25 minutes).
  5. Stir in the granulated sugar and vanilla extract. Taste and adjust the sweetness as needed.
  6. Remove from heat and let the arroz con leche cool slightly.
  7. Serve warm or chilled. Garnish with a sprinkle of ground cinnamon and optional raisins.
